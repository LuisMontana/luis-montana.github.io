Into The Depths

Released on February 28, 2020.

This game was a personal project I developed using Unity. I had the idea of creating a game with as few assets as possible, so I hired my designer friend to create a specific amount of assets to see what I could build with them. I already had work done on the concept for Ludum Dare 43 with a friend and liked the idea enough to extend the development.

The core idea was that to advance to higher levels, you had to sacrifice either your life, your sanity, or your items. With lower sanity levels, enemies and hazards become more dangerous, but with low life, a single mistake could cost you your life. Items were also good to keep around so you could use them in specific situations. The whole idea of "making your own difficulty" has always attracted me, and I've tried to incorporate it into some of my games, but I feel I'm still pulling something that leaves me satisfied

My Contributions:

  • Project Lead: I decided everything besides the art direction and music design. I gave the designer a list of which assets I needed and what type of monsters I wanted, and I gave her creative freedom for the assets as long as they followed the requirements I established.
  • Core gameplay design: After the initial game jam entry, which only used life, I determined that to have more depth, more systems were required. I decided that sanity and items would provide a nice balancing act for players to play the game with a certain level of freedom.
  • Movement System implementation: I had never developed a game with an "isometric" perspective, so I had to get a bit creative with my code to make everything work (even if it didn't work as I intended in some cases).

My learnings and things to improve:

  • The combat system didn't end up as I wanted, and it was easy for the monster that followed you to kind of lock you out of killing them because the diagonal movement made it too difficult to keep them far enough.
  • I would definitely give more time to the level design; they feel too empty and have a feeling of "sameness."
